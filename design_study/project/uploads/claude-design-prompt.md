# Brief

Design a complete, high-fidelity interactive prototype for the personal brand website of Eduardo Neto (digital identity: latticeknight).

This is not a conventional developer portfolio.
It is the home of Eduardo's products, his methods for agentic software development, and his practical view of AI tooling economics.
The work is the protagonist.
Eduardo stays present and human, but the site must never feel self-important or celebrity-focused.

The visitor should leave thinking "this person builds useful products and has a serious, tested way of working with AI agents", not "this person has an elaborate portfolio".

Positioning to communicate: experienced software engineer, systems thinker, builder of real products, explorer of agentic development, practitioner who turns AI subscriptions into reliable engineering workflows, interested in the real economics of AI-assisted engineering rather than hype.

# Brand idea

**Lattice**: interconnected systems, software architecture, agent coordination, dependencies, structured complexity, ideas becoming working systems.

**Knight**: integrity, responsibility, resilience, protecting quality, taking unconventional but purposeful paths.

Express both through structure, behaviour and motion, never through literal symbols.

Hard bans: chess pieces, horses, medieval imagery, swords, shields, hacker or Matrix imagery, crypto aesthetics, glowing brains, floating source code, meaningless particles, excessive neon, oversized portraits, grandiose founder language, repeated "I build / I believe / I am" statements.

# Design direction (committed)

One idea drives everything: **the site is a living lattice**.
A persistent connected graph is the visual and navigational language of the entire experience, not a decorative background.
Pages are regions of one system, and navigation is travel between them.

Aesthetic: dark, architectural, calm, precise, premium, slightly mysterious, technically credible.
Near-black backgrounds, graphite surfaces, warm white typography, restrained cyan and amber accents, thin structural lines, depth through scale, blur and parallax, generous negative space.
Distinctive without becoming a science-fiction film interface.

Lattice behaviour:

- forms clusters around topics and connects related subjects
- reorganises when navigating and illuminates meaningful paths
- reacts subtly to cursor movement
- remembers visited sections during the session and grows richer as the visitor explores

# Opening experience

First visit only: an optional 4-6 second intro with an immediately visible "Skip intro" control.
Near darkness, the lattice awakens, connections form, and the name emerges:

Eduardo Neto
latticeknight

Then one restrained statement:

ENGINEERING WITH AGENTS.
BUILDING SYSTEMS THAT WORK.

The intro must transition seamlessly into the interactive homepage, as though the animation has become the website.
It never replays on later navigation; the lattice simply remains the ambient system.
If a lattice video is supplied, use it as this opening visual; otherwise render the lattice procedurally.

Hero copy (keep it this sparse):

Supporting line: "Exploring how Codex, Claude and automation combine into reliable software-development workflows, and applying those methods to real products."

Primary calls to action: Explore the systems · See the workflows · Read the field notes

# Pages

Use plain human-readable navigation labels; each page may carry a more distinctive internal title.

**1. Home - "The Lattice"**
Concise positioning, current active projects, one featured workflow or experiment, latest field note, a visual map showing how products, agents, automation and engineering thinking connect, and a quiet availability note for suitable roles or collaborations.
It must not resemble a grid of portfolio cards; the lattice itself reveals the main routes.

**2. Systems - "Built in the Real World"**
Products presented as evolving systems, not case-study trophies.
Status vocabulary used consistently: live / in development / experimental / planned.
Each system is a cluster containing: product, architecture, agents, automation, commercial model, open questions, lessons.

- **FoundMyPro** (live): a UK local-services discovery platform focused initially on growth, search authority and affiliate revenue rather than subscriptions.
  Cover: the product problem, discovery and search experience, provider and service data, local SEO, affiliate monetisation, automation opportunities, production decisions, lessons from operating a real platform, and the trade-offs between ambition, resources and speed.
- **Sentinel for Sentry** (in development): presented honestly as earlier-stage work with heavier liability, trust and operational considerations.
  Cover: the intended problem, system concept, risk and reliability, legal and business constraints, why responsible product design sometimes means delaying launch, and how its architecture and automation strategy differ from FoundMyPro.

Never invent metrics, customers, revenue or features.

**3. Agentic SDLC - "Engineering the Workflow"** (major pillar)
How AI agents participate across the full lifecycle (discovery, requirements, research, architecture, planning, coding, testing, review, debugging, security, documentation, deployment, monitoring, maintenance, retrospectives) without surrendering engineering judgement.
Present the lifecycle as an interactive connected system, not a linear infographic.
Show responsibility moving between: human engineer, primary implementation agent, review agent, research agent, testing and validation tooling, CI/CD, production feedback.
Core message: agentic development is not "ask AI to build the app"; it is the deliberate design of context, constraints, permissions, feedback loops, checkpoints, independent verification, escalation rules and ownership.
Include a section titled **WHERE AGENTS FAIL** covering: confident invention, shallow architecture, local optimisation, context loss, unnecessary rewrites, incomplete verification, excessive tool usage, hidden coupling, security mistakes, agreeing with flawed assumptions.
Tone: experienced and pragmatic, never evangelical.

**4. Workflows - "Agent Operating Patterns"**
A library of reusable patterns.
Each pattern shows: problem, workflow, human responsibility, agent responsibility, validation, risks, and when not to use it.
Include around eight, drawn from: Research → Plan → Challenge → Implement → Review; Architect and Builder separation; independent code-review agent; test-first agent loop; bug reproduction before repair; documentation as executable context; safe database migration; repository onboarding; release-readiness review; human approval gates; multi-agent disagreement resolution; context compression and handover.
The lattice visually routes information through stages and shows failed paths being rejected.

**5. Automation - "Remove the Repetition"**
Scripts, tools and small systems that eliminate repetitive engineering work (repository analysis, environment validation, test orchestration, deployment checks, dependency monitoring, documentation generation, visual regression, issue triage, agent handovers, operational reporting).
Two central ideas: automation is a continuum (manual → assisted → scripted → agent-operated → conditionally autonomous), and not every task deserves an agent, because a deterministic script is often cheaper, safer and better.

**6. AI Tooling - "Value per Subscription"**
Getting maximum value from subscriptions such as Codex and Claude, without affiliate content or brand worship.
Cover: choosing the right model per task, assigning complementary roles, using one agent to challenge another, separating research/implementation/review, managing context deliberately, reusable instructions and skills, cost versus quality, subscription limits, when a local script suffices, when human review remains essential.
Build an interactive comparison based on **roles** (explorer, architect, implementer, reviewer, debugger, documentation partner, browser operator, automation runner) rather than "which model is best" rankings.
Include a calculator-style conceptual tool exploring workflow design by task complexity, risk, repetition, cost sensitivity and required verification.
Use editable placeholders wherever live data would be needed; never present invented prices or capabilities as facts.

**7. Lab - "Experiments in Progress"**
Agent experiments, automation prototypes, UI concepts, small open-source tools, failed experiments and lessons.
Unfinished by design.
Status labels: investigating / prototyping / testing / paused / discarded / shipped.
Discarded ideas are shown as evidence of judgement, not hidden.

**8. Field Notes - "What the Work Teaches"**
Writing, categorised: Agentic Engineering, Software Architecture, Automation, Building Products, AI Economics, Failure Analysis, Practical Experiments, Career and Craft.
Articles are visually connected to the systems and workflows they came from (a FoundMyPro article illuminates the FoundMyPro cluster).
Knowledge should feel cumulative rather than chronological.

**9. About - "The Engineer Behind the System"**
Human and restrained.
Software-engineering background, transition from hospitality leadership into software, enterprise application experience, product-building interests, focus on AI-assisted development, preference for useful systems over demonstrations.
The hospitality background communicates service, standards, leadership, calm under pressure and attention to real operational detail.
A modest portrait at most; no generic autobiographical storytelling.

**10. Contact - "Open a Connection"**
Contact as creating a new node in the lattice.
Routes: engineering roles, product collaboration, technical discussion, project feedback, speaking or writing.
Simple and accessible; no theatrical form that makes communication difficult.

# Navigation and motion

Navigation exists in two forms: a clear conventional navigation for accessibility, and an interactive lattice map for exploration showing current location and related destinations.
Never sacrifice usability for originality.

One transition grammar everywhere: the current cluster contracts, a connecting path illuminates, the camera follows the path, the destination cluster expands, and page content resolves from the structure.
Transitions run 500-900 ms; longer cinematic moments only for major first-time discoveries.
Examples: Home → Systems condenses the lattice into the two product clusters; Systems → Agentic SDLC travels from product nodes into the workflows that built them; Field Notes → project illuminates a route back to the originating system; About → Contact detaches a single node that opens a new connection.

Micro-interactions: nodes respond to cursor proximity, edges strengthen when related content is hovered, clusters breathe slowly, a light signal travels a selected route, navigation history remains faintly visible, failed experiments appear as deliberately terminated paths, human approval points appear as stable anchor nodes.
Status is expressed through structural behaviour, not bright badges.
Nothing wobbles, bounces or spins without meaning.

# Voice and copy

Direct, thoughtful, honest, technically literate, occasionally dry.
Never grandiose, never corporate consultancy, never AI influencer.
Banned words: revolutionising, cutting-edge, game-changing, visionary, thought leader, 10x engineer, unlocking potential, leveraging synergies.

Good: "Agents can produce code quickly. The engineering problem is deciding what they should be allowed to change, how their work is verified and who remains responsible."
Bad: "Transforming software development through revolutionary multi-agent intelligence."

Use first person sparingly; prefer verbs (Building, Exploring, Testing, Operating, Documenting) over declarations beginning with "I".
Write realistic draft copy, never lorem ipsum.
Mark any claim that needs confirmation with [VERIFY].

# Responsive and accessibility

Desktop provides the richest spatial experience; tablet retains connected navigation with reduced depth.
Mobile is a deliberate interpretation, not a compressed desktop animation: simplified lattice, touch-friendly clusters, vertical narrative, reduced motion, strong typography, fast access to projects and writing, no tiny interactive nodes, nothing essential behind hover.

The experience must remain fully usable when motion is disabled, WebGL is unavailable, JavaScript loads slowly, or the visitor uses a keyboard, a screen reader, a weak device or a slow connection.
Provide a strong static visual fallback, excellent text contrast, and never let animation delay access to content.
All motion respects prefers-reduced-motion.

# Prototype scope

Build in full detail: the opening sequence, Home, Systems (both products), Agentic SDLC, Workflows with one pattern fully developed, About, Contact, the lattice map, transitions between at least five pages, mobile behaviour, and the reduced-motion mode.
Automation, AI Tooling, Lab and Field Notes should be genuinely designed but may carry representative content depth.

The result should be memorable because every part belongs to the same idea.
It should not merely look extraordinary; it should behave as though it understands its own structure.
